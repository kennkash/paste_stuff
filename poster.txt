1. Problem Description

As Jira adoption grew, teams increasingly needed a better way to guide users before ticket submission, route requests, and support approval-based workflows. Many tickets were submitted for common questions or known issues that could have been resolved through existing documentation if users were directed there first, while approval workflows were only supported in Jira Service Management (JSM) projects.

This pushed teams toward creating JSM projects even when they only needed lightweight intake, documentation guidance, or approvals. The result was added administrative overhead, license pressure, service project sprawl, and dependence on a third-party plugin for portal customization, impacting request submitters, project teams, and administrators.
⸻

2. Work SMART Method

We studied how teams were handling intake and portal needs in Jira and focused on reducing unnecessary complexity, cost, and wasted effort.

Our approach aligned with Work SMART principles by:
	•	focusing on customer value,
	•	simplifying workflows at the point of activity,
	•	standardizing intake paths,
	•	and reducing waste from extra tools, tickets, and administration.

Before production release, we ran a multi-month pilot in staging so users could test functionality and provide feedback.

⸻

3. Improvement

We developed RAIL, a Jira Data Center plugin that enables project-scoped portals for intake, approvals, documentation, and forms without requiring new JSM projects for lightweight use cases.

RAIL provides reusable portal components, project-level control, and routing to Jira Software or internal forms. Project admins govern their own portal experience.

To sustain the improvement, we maintain compatibility with Jira, Atlassian SDK, and Node.js updates, and provide portal training and documentation in Confluence.

⸻

4. Results

RAIL was deployed across all 104 JSM projects and expanded to 3 software projects with project-scoped portals.

The solution:
	•	reduces dependency on third-party portal tooling,
	•	avoids unnecessary JSM growth,
	•	lowers administrative overhead,
	•	and gives projects more direct ownership of intake and user guidance.

Based on conservative production estimates, RAIL avoids roughly $200K/year in incremental JSM license cost, in addition to eliminating about $16.2K/year in portal plugin cost.

Problem Description

Jira teams needed portals for intake, documentation, and approvals, but the default solution was often a new JSM project. This increased admin effort, license pressure, service project sprawl, and reliance on third-party portal tooling. All Jira users were affected.

Work SMART Method

We analyzed current intake workflows and targeted waste tied to extra tools, excess administration, and unnecessary ticketing complexity. We emphasized customer value, simplified workflows, standardized intake, and validated the approach through a multi-month staging pilot.

Improvement

We built RAIL, a Jira Data Center plugin that enables project-scoped portals for intake, approvals, documentation, and forms. Project admins manage their own portals. Sustainability is supported through platform upkeep, Confluence documentation, and user training.

Results

RAIL was implemented across 104 JSM projects and 3 software projects. It reduces third-party tool dependence, limits unnecessary JSM growth, and lowers admin overhead. Conservative estimates show about $200K/year avoided in JSM license growth plus $16.2K/year in plugin savings.
